Peter is a population health epidemiologist and informaticist whose research focuses on the intersection of exposome, health, and clinical data linkage. He specializes in advanced statistical modeling, opportunistic detection using machine learning, and geospatial analysis with his most recent work developing computational phenotypes to assess the impact of environmental exposures on cardiovascular disease outcomes in Chicago. His work integrates multimodal data sources for risk prediction to improve clinical risk prediction, public health surveillance, and precision prevention strategies.
