Dr. William F. Parker is an intensivist, clinical medical ethicist, and health services researcher who leads the Parker Healthcare Allocation (HCA) Lab at the University of Chicago. His work focuses on the algorithmic allocation of scarce healthcare resources, including deceased donor organ allocation, life-support triage under crisis standards of care, and novel scarce therapeutics.

He is also the founding executive director of the Common Longitudinal ICU data Format (CLIF) consortium.
